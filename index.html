What is Force?

Force is a vector quantity that represents the interaction between objects. It has both magnitude and direction, and it can cause objects to accelerate, decelerate, or change direction.

It is basically a push or pull on an object

Balanced and Unbalanced forces

When an object recieves equal magnitude of force in opposite directions, it is called a balanced force.
For example, A box kept on a floor.
What happens here is that the box exerts a force on the floor and the floor exerts equal force on the box.


When an object recieves unequal magnitude of force in opposite directions, it is called an unbalanced force.
For example, A man pushing a box.
What happens here is that the man exerts a force on the box but the box moves as there is no force resisting it on the box

Formulae and Newton's Laws of Motion

Force

F = m × a

Where:

F = Force (Newtons, N)

m = Mass (Kilograms, kg)

a = Acceleration (meters per second squared, m/s2)

Newton's First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

Newton's Second Law of Motion

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. The equation is given by:

F = m × a

Newton's Third Law of Motion

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Momentum

Momentum is a fundamental concept in physics that quantifies the motion possessed by an object. It is defined as the product of an object's mass and velocity.

Formula for momentum

p = m × v

Where:

p = Momentum(Kilogram Metre per second / kgms-1)

m = Mass(kg)

v = velocity(metre per second/ms-1)

Conservation of Momentum

Conservation of momentum is a major law of physics which states that the momentum of a system is constant if no external forces are acting on the system.

Formula

Take an object A with:
mass m1
initial velocity u1
final velocity v1

Take an object B with:
mass m2
initial velocity u2
final velocity v2

The change in momentum for object A would be m1v1 - m1u1
and the change in momentum for object B would be m2v2 - m2u2

From the Third Law of Motion, we know that

FBA = -FAB

Hence m1(v1 - u1)/t = -m2(v2 - u2)/t, resulting in
m1u1 + m1v1 = m2u2 + m2v2
